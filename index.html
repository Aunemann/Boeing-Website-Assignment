INFO

  • Boeing's first name was Pacific Aero Products Company and was founded in 1916 by William Boeing.
  • In 1917 Boeing was renamed to Boeing Airplane Company or Boeing for short.
  • In 1997 Boeing merged with MacDonnel Douglas. MacDonnel Douglas is known for making aircraft such as the DC-10, C-17 Globemaster and the A-4 Skyhawk. This merge lead to significant changes in corporate operations. The DC-10 has gotten the name Donald's Disaster and Flying Coffin in it's first few years of service.

FINANCIAL

  • Boeing is a major global aerospace manufacturer and the largest exporter in the United States by dollar value.
  • Reported $62.3 billion in sales in 2021, ranking among the largest aerospace companies globally.

737 MAX CRISIS

  • Experienced two fatal crashes involving the Boeing 737 MAX in 2018 and 2019, resulting in global groundings and significant scrutiny.
  • Investigations revealed issues with design flaws, regulatory violations, and internal culture affecting safety standards.

LEGAL

  • Boeing faced legal actions, settlements, and fines related to the 737 MAX crashes, including fraud charges and compensations to stakeholders and victims.
  • Ongoing legal disputes, such as claims challenging the emotional distress experienced by victims of the crashes.

PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

  • Despite ongoing challenges, Boeing continued with new aircraft development, including the Boeing 777X, which faced delays and technical issues.
